Consulting Process

1. Stakeholder Debriefing

  • Comprehensive audit of the sales department and current performance indicators
  • Strategy development
  • Description of sales funnels, identification of key measurable performance indicators

2. Clarification and Alignment

  • Team roster creation
  • Defining necessary roles and competencies within the team
  • Analysis of current staff and identification of specialist gaps

3. Personnel Recruitment

  • Assistance in finding specialists to fill identified staffing needs
  • Utilizing professional contacts and channels for employee recruitment
  • Creating test assignments when necessary

4. Sales Department Structure

  • Organizing sales department operations, including development of training materials and analytical reporting
  • Development of sales scenarios and scripts
  • Setting up regular training and quality assessment of the department
  • Integration of customer service and after-sales support processes

5. Interaction and Optimization

  • Supporting the change process and interaction with related departments
  • Ensuring regular interaction between sales and marketing departments

6. Project Completion and Handover

  • Gradual reduction of project involvement with transfer of responsibilities to the client's team
  • Achieving a state where the company can operate effectively independently

Work Philosophy

A key principle in my consulting philosophy is that the ultimate goal of a consultant is to make herself redundant. The work is considered successful when the client's team can confidently continue along the implemented path without my support.
